# PowerUp — Complete All-Time Franchise Rosters
**Version:** 2.0 — Full 30-team build
**Rule:** One primary franchise per player except Babe Ruth (pitcher on Red Sox, hitter on Yankees + World team)
**Aura rule:** Stats are necessary but not sufficient. Cultural weight, mythology, and franchise identity count equally.
**Banned/ineligible note:** Shoeless Joe Jackson and Pete Rose included on their primary teams. Their mythology IS their franchises. PED-era players included where their legacy is inseparable from franchise identity (Bonds/Giants, Sosa/Cubs).
**Generate at:** Each player's peak season with that franchise unless noted.

## NOTES ON CONTROVERSIAL INCLUSIONS

**Pete Rose** (Reds): All-time hits leader. Banned for gambling. His aura IS the Reds and baseball's hit record. Generate at 1969 peak (3B) — the hustle years, before the gambling era.

**Shoeless Joe Jackson** (White Sox): Third-highest career BA in history. Banned for alleged 1919 fix (his stats in that WS were excellent). His mythology IS the Black Sox story. Generate at 1920 peak.

**Barry Bonds** (Giants): Greatest offensive seasons in history. Generate at 2001–04 peak. His presence on the Giants roster is non-negotiable regardless of context.

**Sammy Sosa** (Cubs): 1998 home run race. Cultural moment. Generate at 1998 peak. The corked bat and PED implications are part of the mythology.

**Bobby Bonilla** (Mets): Not an all-time great by stats. His annual $1.19M Mets deferred payment (Bobby Bonilla Day, July 1) is one of the great baseball stories. Included as a mythology pick.

**Joe Carter** (Blue Jays): One of three players ever to win a World Series with a walk-off HR. Generate for that hit.

**Bill Mazeroski** (Pirates): Only 2B ever to win a World Series with a walk-off HR (also only the second walk-off HR in WS history at the time). Generate for that swing.

**Babe Ruth (pitcher)** (Red Sox): Appears twice in the entire project — pitcher on Red Sox, hitter on Yankees and All-Time World. The Curse of the Bambino is inseparable from both franchises. This is the one deliberate two-team exception.
